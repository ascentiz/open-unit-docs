# University Labs

This page is intended for university groups exploring collaboration with Ascentiz around exoskeleton controls, sensing, and applied human-machine systems research.

## Collaboration Opportunities

Typical collaboration areas may include:

- gait assistance and rehabilitation studies
- intent estimation and adaptive control research
- sensor fusion and human state estimation
- wearable robotics evaluation protocols
- telemetry analysis and longitudinal study tooling

## What A Lab Partnership Might Include

Depending on maturity and scope, a collaboration may involve:

- access to an Open Unit development kit
- shared interface documentation and integration support
- joint definition of experiment requirements and safety assumptions
- structured telemetry export for analysis workflows

## What Ascentiz Needs From Research Partners

To make collaboration productive, partner labs should be ready to provide:

- a clear technical objective
- the expected hardware and sensing setup
- named points of contact for controls, software, and study operations
- a realistic plan for lab safety review and operator training

## Good Early Conversations To Have

Before starting integration, align on:

- which parts of the stack are fixed versus experimental
- what telemetry is required for evaluation
- which performance metrics matter most
- how failures, anomalies, and device limits will be handled

## Publication And IP

Publication, data rights, and intellectual property terms depend on the collaboration model and should be handled explicitly rather than assumed.

## Contact Workflow

Until a dedicated research intake channel exists, labs should open a structured inquiry through the process described in [Community > Feedback](../community/feedback.md).
